WEBINARS

Check out our selection of video workshops and presentations hosted online regarding Parenting and Child Protection resources. These sessions are used to share knowledge, ideas, and updates with people around the world working on Parenting and Child Protection projects during COVID-19.

WEBINAR: COVID-19 & Child Protection (April 2020)

The Global Partnership to End Violence Against Children (EVAC) and the Early Childhood Development Action Network (ECDAN) partnered to present a webinar focused on key issues on Child Protection in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic. The webinar provided an overview of the issue of Child Protection, with lessons learned from past emergency contexts, current data on the prevalence of violence against children, presentation of guides and strategies to promote the protection of children and country presentations on strategies being currently implemented.

WEBINAR: SEMINARIO IBEROAMERICANO FAN FUNDACION Y EARLY INSTITUTE DIA 4

Presentation of our free parenting resources delivered by Brigitte De Hulsters at the Seminario Iberamericano last week (beginning at 59:34).

The Iberoamerican Seminar for Prevention, detection and intervention in cases of sexual violence in children and adolescents in Latin America and the Caribean (LAC) was organized by FAN (Fundacion de Atencion a la Ninez, Colombia) and Early Institute (Mexico) from 25 till 28 august 2020 and counted presentations by a representative of ISPCAN, on the use of data to prevent violence in children/adolescents, of Early Institute, and ALUMBRA, Mexico, on the creation of alliances between governments and organizations on this topic, of the Government of Gran Canarias, Spain, on the pilot multisectoral approach that harmonizes the rights of both victim and accused and avoids revictimization of the child; PAICABI, a Chilean NGO, and Jugar para Sanar (FAN Colombia), on the need to restore the health WITH (not OF) the child, humanizing surroundings, protective and therapeutic interventions repairing (not focusing on the damage), the perspectives and continuing development of the child. COVID19parenting was the closing presentation, and received much interest as a true prevention method now. Directors of FAN and Early considered this first seminar a great success. Attendant’s count was above 400 for each session. Inscriptions 554. Certain voids of multicultural knowledge and practices were closed. Knowledge sharing was effective and emphasized that the restoration of the child or adolescent after abuse, needs to happen WITH the child/adolescent, through play, avoiding revictimization and by a multisectoral team in appropriate surroundings for the victim. Prevention is always better, and parents need access to tools for play and stress reduction to avoid intrafamily violence directed towards children, especially during COVID19.

WEBINAR: Beyond Masks: Societal impacts of COVID -19 and accelerated solutions for children and adolescents (November 2020)

UNICEF Innocenti's new report – Beyond Masks: Societal impacts of COVID-19 and accelerated solutions for children and adolescents – offers a comprehensive picture of the health, economic, and social impacts of the pandemic, and its implications for children and adolescents. The report examines evidence from the current crisis, examines past health crises such as HIV/AIDS, SARS and Ebola to provide insights into the current one, and proposes proven and promising solutions.

This panel discussion, timed with the launch of the Beyond Masks report on 19 November, 2020, comes at a moment when policymakers are asking deep questions about how to deal with the raging pandemic in many parts of the world while ensuring children’s health, education and well-being. In it, we delve deep into Beyond Masks to better understand how its findings can shape national and subnational policy responses and individual, family and community behaviors. With COVID-19 pandemic showing no signs of abating, there is an urgent need to find scalable and cost-effective solutions to the continued and deepening impact of the COVID crisis on the world’s youngest citizens.
